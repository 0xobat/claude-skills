## Reply Maximizers

### 1. The Open Question

**Purpose**: Maximize P(reply) through direct invitation

**Format**:
```
[Specific observation or claim] (40-80 chars)

[Optional: Context or your answer first] (0-100 chars)

What's your [specific experience]? (25-40 chars)
```

**Optimal length**: 100-180 characters total

**Example**:
```
I've talked to 50+ founders this year. The #1 regret?

Hiring too fast when they hit product-market fit.

What's the worst hiring mistake you've made?
```

**Why it works**:
- Question demands a response (not rhetorical)
- Specificity triggers relevant memories
- Personal framing invites personal responses
- Going first models the expected response

**Algorithm alignment**:
- P(reply): ‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë (15-25% expected)
- P(like): ‚Üë‚Üë
- P(block): ‚Üì‚Üì (non-controversial)
- **Est. score contribution**: +2.0 to +3.5

**Variations**:
- "What's your biggest [X] failure?"
- "What [tool/book/habit] changed everything for you?"
- "What do you wish you'd learned earlier about [X]?"

### 2. The Fill-in-the-Blank

**Purpose**: Lowest friction reply format (highest P(reply) potential)

**Format**:
```

**Optimal length**: 60-100 characters total

**Example**:

**Why it works**:
- Near-zero cognitive load to reply
- Format is inherently incomplete ‚Üí demands completion

**Algorithm alignment**:
- P(reply): ‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë (20-35% expected‚Äîhighest of any format)

### 3. The Correctability Hook

**Purpose**: Trigger expert responses by being slightly wrong

**Format**:
```
[Statement that's 80% correct but has a notable flaw]

[Optional: invitation for pushback]
```

**Optimal length**: 80-150 characters

**Example**:
```
The best way to learn programming is to build projects, not take courses.

Books are basically useless for technical skills.
```

**Why it works**:
- Experts can't resist correcting perceived errors
- "Well, actually..." impulse drives high-quality replies
- Creates genuine debate, not manufactured controversy
- Replies from experts boost your content's credibility

**Algorithm alignment**:
- P(reply): ‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë (from experts wanting to correct)
- P(quote): ‚Üë‚Üë (experts quote to add nuance)
- P(block): ‚Üë (slight risk from those who strongly disagree)
- **Est. score contribution**: +1.5 to +3.0

**Caution**: Don't be wrong about sensitive topics (politics, health, etc.)‚Äîstick to professional/technical domains.

## Media Optimizers

### 9. The Visual Framework

**Purpose**: Trigger P(photo_expand) through intentionally complex visuals

**Format**:
```
[Intriguing claim or question] (40-80 chars)

**Image specs**:
- Vertical (4:5 or 9:16) to force crop

```

**Why it works**:
- Visual pattern interrupt in text-heavy feed
- Cropped image creates curiosity ‚Üí photo_expand
- Complex visual requires inspection time
- Question converts viewers to repliers

**Algorithm alignment**:
- P(photo_expand): ‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë (forces click to see full image)
- P(reply): ‚Üë‚Üë (if question included)
- P(repost): ‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë (useful visuals are shared)
- **Est. score contribution**: +1.5 to +2.5 (with media bonus)

### 10. The Video Hook

**Purpose**: Maximize P(video_view) through first-second optimization

**Format**:

```

**Why it works**:
- Video gets visual priority in feed
- Early hook captures attention before scroll
- Captions enable silent consumption
- Native upload (not links) triggers P(video_view)

**Algorithm alignment**:
- P(video_view): ‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë‚Üë (if hook works)

## High-Risk / High-Reward

**Why it works**:
- "Unpopular opinion" frames as discussion, not attack

**Critical**: Add nuance. Pure contrarian without reasoning = high P(block).

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### The Link Dump
```

```
**Why it fails**:
- No media probability terms
- No reply hook
- No value in the tweet itself
- P(click) has low weight anyway
- **Est. score: NEGATIVE**

### The Vague Question
```
‚ùå What do you think?
```
**Why it fails**:
- No specificity = no trigger for response
- Feels lazy
- **Est. score contribution: ~+0.2**

### The Thread Tease
```
‚ùå Thread! üßµ

Here are 10 tips for X...

1/ First tip...
```
**Why it fails**:
- Tweet 1 has no standalone value
- "Thread!" signals spam to Grok
- **Est. score contribution: ~+0.3 to negative**

### The Rage-Bait
```
‚ùå [Inflammatory statement designed to anger]
```
**Why it fails**:
- High engagement but high P(block)
- Negative weights destroy positive gains
- Cluster reputation damage
- **Est. score: NEGATIVE (often severely)**

## Optimal Posting Specs

### Character Counts
- **Sweet spot**: 71-100 characters (highest engagement per character)
- **Safe max**: 280 characters (no "Show more")
- **Extended**: 280+ only for high-value lists/threads

### Hashtag Strategy
- **0-1 hashtags** optimal
- Must semantically match content (Grok checks)
- Trending hijacking = spam signal

### Media Specs
- **Images**: 1200√ó675px (1.91:1) or vertical for crop trick
- **Video**: Under 2:20, hook in first 2 seconds, captions required
- **Always native upload** (links don't trigger media probabilities)

### Timing
- Post when your audience is active
- Space posts 4-6+ hours apart (author diversity penalty)
- Quality over quantity is mathematically enforced
